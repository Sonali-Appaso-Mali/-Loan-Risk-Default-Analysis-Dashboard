## 📌 Project Overview

The **Loan Risk & Default Analysis Dashboard** is an end-to-end **Data Analytics** project designed to analyze loan applications, borrower demographics, financial behavior, and default risk.

The project follows a complete analytics workflow starting from **Microsoft Excel** for raw data handling, **SQL Server** for data storage, **Power Query** for transformation, **DAX** for business calculations, and **Power BI** for creating an interactive dashboard.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor lending performance, understand borrower characteristics, identify financial risk patterns, and support informed business decisions through data visualization.

# 🎯 Business Problem

Financial institutions receive thousands of loan applications from customers with diverse financial backgrounds. Evaluating loan eligibility while minimizing the risk of loan defaults is a significant challenge.

Without proper analysis, lenders may face:

- High loan default rates
- Increased financial losses
- Inefficient credit risk assessment
- Poor lending decisions
- Limited visibility into borrower behavior

This project addresses these challenges by transforming raw loan data into meaningful insights using interactive Power BI dashboards, enabling better risk assessment and data-driven decision-making.

---

# 🎯 Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are:

- Analyze loan distribution across different loan purposes.
- Evaluate applicant demographics and financial profiles.
- Monitor loan default trends over time.
- Identify high-risk borrower segments.
- Analyze financial risk metrics using interactive visualizations.
- Support business decisions with actionable insights.
- Build an end-to-end Business Intelligence dashboard using Microsoft Power BI.

# 💡 Key Business Insights

The dashboard highlights several important patterns related to loan distribution, borrower characteristics, and financial risk.

## 📊 Loan Portfolio Insights

- Home loans account for the largest share of the total loan amount, indicating that housing finance represents the primary lending segment.
- Business and Education loans also contribute significantly to the overall loan portfolio.
- Loans categorized as **Other** represent the smallest lending segment.

---

## 👨‍💼 Employment Analysis

- Full-time employees have the highest average income among all employment categories.
- Loan default rates vary across different employment types, highlighting employment status as an important risk indicator.
- Employment information can be effectively used to strengthen borrower risk assessment during loan approval.

---

## 👥 Applicant Demographics

- Adult applicants receive the highest average loan amounts compared to younger age groups.
- Loan distribution differs across education levels, with Bachelor's degree holders representing a significant portion of applicants.
- Marital status and age group influence borrowing patterns and loan allocation.

---

## 💳 Credit Risk Analysis

- Credit score categories show clear differences in loan distribution.
- Borrowers with different credit profiles contribute differently to the overall lending portfolio.
- Credit score remains one of the most important indicators for evaluating lending risk.

---

## 📈 Financial Performance

- Loan amounts fluctuate across different years, reflecting changes in lending activity over time.
- Default trends also vary yearly, emphasizing the need for continuous monitoring of portfolio performance.
- High-income applicants contribute a significant share of the overall loan amount.

---

## 📌 Overall Business Insight

The dashboard demonstrates that loan performance is influenced by multiple factors including employment type, income level, applicant demographics, credit score, and loan purpose. Combining these dimensions provides a more comprehensive view of lending risk and supports better business decision-making.

---
# 🚀 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations can help improve lending performance and reduce financial risk.

## 1. Strengthen Credit Risk Assessment

Prioritize detailed credit evaluation for applicants with higher-risk financial profiles before loan approval.

---

## 2. Improve Employment-Based Risk Analysis

Include employment type as an important factor in credit assessment since default rates differ across employment categories.

---

## 3. Enhance Portfolio Monitoring

Continuously monitor yearly loan performance and default trends to identify emerging risks at an early stage.

---

## 4. Promote Risk-Based Lending

Adopt risk-based lending strategies by considering credit score, income level, and financial profile together.

---

## 5. Improve Customer Segmentation

Segment borrowers using demographic characteristics such as age, education, marital status, and employment to design better lending strategies.

---

## 6. Expand Dashboard Monitoring

Regularly update the dashboard with new loan data to support real-time business intelligence and decision-making.

---

## 7. Data-Driven Decision Making

Use interactive Power BI dashboards to support lending decisions with measurable insights rather than relying solely on manual assessment.
